    Dermatology

    Dermatology Treatment

    Dermatology encompasses the diagnosis and treatment of various conditions related to the skin, hair, and nails. There are several treatments available in dermatology, depending on the specific condition being addressed.

    Book an Appointment

    1. Topical medications:
      Dermatologists of ten prescribe topical. creams, gels, ointments, or lotions to treat various skin conditions such as acne, eczema, psoriasis, fungal infections, and dermatitis. These medications are applied directly to the affected area of the skin.
    2. Oral medications :
      In some cases, dermatologist may Prescribe oral medications to manage certain skin conditions. For example antihistamines for allergic reactions, or antifungal drugs for systemic fungal infections.
    3. Phototherapy:
      phototherapy involves exposing the skin to controlled amounts of ultraviolet (UV) light to treat conditions like psoriasis, vitiligo and eczema. Different types of phototherapy include narrow-band UVB, broadband UVB, and psoralen plus ultraviolet A(PUVA) therapy
    4. Cryotherapy:
      Cryotherapy involves the use of extreme cold temperatures to freeze and destroy abnormal skin cells or lesions, such as warts, actinic keratosis (Precancerous skin lesions), and some skin cancers. Liquid nitrogen is typically used in this procedures.
    5. surgical procedures:
      Dermatologists Perform various surgical procedures to remove skin growth, tumors, or cancers. There may include excision (cutting out the lesion), Mohs surgery
    6. chemical peels:
      chemical peels involve applying a chemical solution to the skin, which Causes exfoliation and subsequen regeneration of new skin cells. They are commonly used to improve the appearance of sun-damaged skin, acne scars,fine lines, and wrinkles.
    7. Laser therapy:
      Laser treatments w–µ focused beams of light to target specific skin concerns. They can be used for various purposes, such as hair removal, tattoo removal, scar reduction, skin rejuvenation, and treatment of vascular lession or pigmented lesions
    8. Injectable treatment:
      Dermatologists injectables may administer injectables treatments like Botox or dermal fillers to reduce the apperance of wrinkles, fine lines, and facial volume loss. These treatment are typically used for cosmetic purpose.
